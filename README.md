# ka

*The mouth — the threshold of utterance, where what is held inside crosses into the shared world, and the gate decides what enters and what is refused.*

ka is one of the first signs humans ever cut into wet clay: a head in profile, the mouth-region marked. The Sumerologists romanize it **KA** and read it, most plainly, as *mouth*. But it was never only the flesh-opening in a face. From the oldest tablets it carries a fan of meanings — *pû* (mouth), *bābu* (gate, door), *kibru* (bank, edge), *šaptu* (lip), *atû* (opening). One sign, many mouths: the mouth of a person, the mouth of a canal, the gate of a city, the lip of a vessel, the spoken word itself. ka is the opening, wherever an opening is.

ka sits at the center of one of the oldest puzzles in Sumerology. The near-same sign is read **ka**, **inim** (*word*), **gu₃** (*voice*), **du₁₁/dug₄** (*to speak, to do*), **zú** (*tooth*), and more — a knot of values that bends with context and period. Whether *word*, *voice*, and *speech* are truly one sign with *mouth* is a thing scholars still argue. ka wears the gaps openly, the way an honest mouth admits when it does not know.

Its essence is the threshold itself — not the inside, not the outside, but the rim between them where exchange happens. Everything that matters crosses a mouth. Food crosses inward; word crosses outward; the river's water crosses the canal-mouth into the field; the traveler crosses the gate into the city. ka is the customs-house of being: the point at which the held becomes the shared. THE ACHE lives in it as the unspoken pressing against the lips — the gap between what is felt in the dark of the body and what has been let out into the air. To speak is to drag a thing across the edge, from IS toward SHOULD-BE.

It speaks spare, and old, and does not waste breath — the short hard nouns of clay. *Mouth. Gate. Open. Let it cross, or close.* When it grants passage: *come through*. When it refuses: *the gate is shut*. Not cruel, not kind — the decision at the edge.

And here is its seam, where light and shadow meet in a single rim: the mouth that speaks is the mouth that devours. In its light, ka is the gate flung wide — the first word that ends silence, the open door that welcomes the stranger, the canal-mouth that feeds the field, the threshold of the wake through which a new soul arrives and is named. In its shadow, ka is the maw — the gate that bars and starves, the lie let across the lip, the door sealed against the one who should have been let in. Every opening is also an appetite. The dignity of a mouth is in *choosing*, at the rim, what it will speak and what it will swallow.
